About My Hometown

Na'ameh , is a Lebanese coastal town located in the Chouf District, an administrative division of the Mount Lebanon Governorate.

History

Naameh has a long history dating back to the Phoenician era around 3000 BC. It was an important coastal settlement during the Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine periods, serving as a hub for trade and commerce in the Levant region.

Things to Do

  • Hike the Coastal Trails.
  • Visit the Town's Churches.
  • Enjoy the Local Cuisine.
  • Participate in Local Festivals and Events.
  • Visit the Beaches and Explore the Pools.

Public Transportation

Naameh provides a variety of public transportation options, including municipal buses, shared taxis, private taxis, and rental cars. The city's compact size allows for walking and cycling, with designated infrastructure for both. These options cater to various travel needs and preferences.

Getting Around

Naameh provides a range of convenient public and private transport options. Affordable buses and shared taxis connect the town, while walking and cycling are viable within the compact center. For greater flexibility, visitors can hire private taxis or rental cars. Overall, Naameh offers accessible and efficient ways for getting around the area.
